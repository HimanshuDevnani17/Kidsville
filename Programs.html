OUR PROGRAMS


Kidz Villa programs are filled with investigation, exploration and discovery. Villa introduces language, numeracy, science and social skills in a natural sequence to nourish learning one step at a time, with individual attention suited to a child’s unique need. Our programs are aimed to address holistic development of a child. Children pursue individual learning pathways that reflect their interests, develop skills for the future and inspire global citizenship.

Each day is packed with Villa Moments™ with carefully designed activities structured around the seven main developmental domains.


Parent Toddler Program


A 6-month program for 10 months + infants specially designed to help toddlers prepare for ‘Pre-School’ phase. It is a program for parent and toddler to get an overview of the activities done in the preschool. The program ensures a smooth transition of toddler in a preschool. It’s a series of sessions spread over weeks, sometimes months to make children comfortable with the preschool environment. The program is full of fun and learning, age appropriate activities to make sure that the toddlers attending are not intimidated by the idea of school but actually enjoy going to school.


Toddler Program


A yearly program for 2 years + children specially designed in an informal warm and secured homely environment where different activities are introduced to develop their gross motor and fine motor skills, and also to enhance their vocabulary through rhymes and stories. We look forward to prepare them for further schooling, all in a relaxed and friendly environment.

Pre-Kindergarten Program (Nursery)


A yearly program for 3 years + children designed to spend four hours a day at school immersed in active and purposeful learning. They are introduced to literacy, numeracy, understanding the world around them, science, art & craft through child-initiated activities under the guidance of an experienced teacher. Our curriculum is designed to provide a safe environment with equal opportunities so as to empower and educate. Our program is based on research that proves children learn best through repetition and practice. It offers children a variety of activities within a carefully prepared indoor and outdoor environment. Students experience a sense of belonging in a caring learning environment that addresses their well-being.


Junior Kindergarten


A yearly program for 4 years + children designed to introduce literacy wherein they learn to read through jolly phonics and numeracy to learn pre-math concepts and number values. Children explore natural resources through practical learning. Our kindergarten program focuses on mastering kindergarten-level math, reading and science objectives. Our curriculum is based upon maximizing child’s potential through self-development programs like yoga, exercises, sports, recreation and field visits. Our teachers continuously work on creating relationships, dialogues, atmosphere of learning through matured, affectionate and stable mentoring.

Senior Kindergarten


A yearly program for 5 years + children to enrich them with rich, meaningful and balanced curriculum of skills and information through age-appropriate activities. Children develop literacy skills, numeracy skills, scientific skills & historical knowledge. They gain confidence and a feeling of self-worth; develop responsibility and independence, learn social skills such as listening and respecting people and resources, develop team work, build self-awareness and learn to be creative.


Day Care Centre & Activity Centre


Day Care Centre, a home away from home, provides care, warmth and homely environment to toddlers, preschoolers, and school-aged children. It is specially designed for working parents who want their child to be served warm food, have a cozy noon nap, get playtime with peers, gets help in homework and can do extra co-curricular activities. Activity Centre essentially takes place outside a typical pen and pencil classroom experience. It gives the students an opportunity to develop particular skills and exhibit their non-academic abilities, such as music, art or drama classes. These centres groom children in the ‘Art of living and working together’. They are the true and practical experiences gained by students and by their own learning. The aesthetic development like character building, spiritual and moral values, physical growth, creativity and many more are backed up with such activities.
